## Quick orientation
Jeju is an oval. Plan by side to avoid backtracking — Jeju City ↔ Seogwipo is ~50–60 min by car. [2][3]
- **North** = Jeju City (airport, downtown, Dongmun Market)
- **South** = Seogwipo (Jungmun resort zone, waterfalls, Olle Market)
- **West** = Aewol / Hallim (cafe street, beaches)
- **East** = Seongsan / Udo / Hamdeok (sunrise peak, Manjanggul, Woljeongri)
- **Center** = Hallasan, Sangumburi, Osulloc

**Suggested base split (5D4N):** 2 nights Jeju City (do North + East), then 2 nights Seogwipo/Jungmun (do South + West). [3]

## ✅ Practical notes (from tips posts)
- **Apps:** Naver Map (search in Korean), T-Map for driving, Kakao T for taxis, Papago translate, CatchTable for restaurant reservations. [2][6][8]
- **Car:** book the rental early in peak season; group each day's stops on one side of the island. [2]
- **Money:** tax-refund threshold 15,000 KRW; convenience stores/markets are pricey; WOWPASS handy. [2][6][19]
- **Entry / timing:** most attractions charge entry; Hallasan & Seongsan have strict early-access windows; many markets/restaurants close Tue/Wed. [2][3]
- **Souvenirs:** Hallabong tangerine products (chocolate, jam, jelly, makgeolli) — buy processed (fresh fruit can't be carried); Jeju-limited tangerine yogurt at CU/7-Eleven; Jeju-limited UNIQLO tee. [3][16][21]
- **Seasonal (June):** fresh tangerine season is over — buy processed; hydrangeas in bloom (Jongdal-ri). [2][3][11]
- **Activity:** clear-bottom speedboat experience mentioned (details in a follow-up vlog). [19]
